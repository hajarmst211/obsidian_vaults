A speaker must clearly and concisely state what the speech is about. Failing to do so leads to audience confusion and a loss of interest.

*   **Credibility:** The speaker should explain why they are qualified to speak on the topic (e.g., personal experience or extensive research).

Here is a summary of the key points:

### The Purpose of an Introduction
A strong introduction is essential for making a good first impression, building the speaker’s self-confidence, and ensuring the audience is mentally prepared. It has four main objectives:
1.  **Get attention and interest.**
2.  **Reveal the topic.**
3.  **Establish credibility and goodwill.**
4.  **Preview the body of the speech.**

### Methods for Getting Attention
The text outlines seven specific techniques to hook the audience:
*   **Relate the topic to the audience:** Show the listeners how the subject affects them personally.
*   **State the importance of your topic:** Use facts or statistics to demonstrate why the subject matters.
*   **Startle the audience:** Use a shocking or intriguing statement to grab immediate focus.
*   **Arouse curiosity:** Build suspense with a series of statements that lead up to the topic.
*   **Question the audience:** Use rhetorical questions to get listeners thinking.
*   **Begin with a quotation:** Use a short, impactful quote (humorous or serious).
*   **Tell a story:** Use a dramatic or personal anecdote to create an emotional connection.

### Revealing the Topic and Establishing Credibility
*   **Be Clear:** A speaker must state the topic directly to avoid confusing the audience. "Beating around the bush" can cause listeners to lose interest.
*   **Credibility:** This is the audience’s perception of whether a speaker is qualified. A speaker should briefly explain their experience or research on the topic to gain trust.
*   **Goodwill:** Especially in controversial speeches, the speaker must show they have the audience's best interests at heart.

### Delivery Tips
*   **Wait for silence:** Before speaking, stand at the lectern and wait for the audience to settle.
*   **Eye Contact:** Effective introductions are delivered with strong eye contact.
*   **Proportion:** An introduction should generally account for **10–20 percent** of the total speech.
