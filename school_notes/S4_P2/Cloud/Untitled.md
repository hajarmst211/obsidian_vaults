### **Cloud Provider Comparison: AWS vs. Azure vs. GCP**

| Category | **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** | **Microsoft Azure** | **Google Cloud Platform (GCP)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **History** | Launched in 2006; the oldest and most established. | Launched in 2010. | Launched in 2011. |
| **Market Position** | Largest market share (approx. 34%). | Strong second place; focus on Hybrid Cloud. | Focused on Big Data and Open Source. |
| **Pricing** | Vast array of services, but structure can be complex to navigate. | Straightforward pricing with discounts for long-term commitments. | Reputation for being the most cost-effective of the three. |
| **User Interface** | Comprehensive console, but can be overwhelming for beginners. | Considered intuitive with a streamlined interface for Microsoft users. | Rated the most user-friendly and beginner-friendly interface. |
| **Service Range** | Most comprehensive offering; highly customizable for any need. | Solid selection tailored for Enterprise customers. | Smaller, more focused range of services for developers. |
| **Specialty Areas** | **IoT:** Most comprehensive set of services available. | **Enterprise:** Seamless integration with Office 365 and Dynamics. | **Data/ML:** Industry leader in Big Data, Analytics, and AI (TensorFlow). |
| **Databases** | Offers "Purpose-Built" databases for every possible use case. | Strong SQL and NoSQL (Cosmos DB) offerings. | Focused on global scale and speed (Spanner, Bigtable). |
| **No-Code Tools** | App Runner, CodeStar, and Honeycode. | Logic Apps and Power Automate. | Cloud Dataflow (focused primarily on data pipelines). |
| **Best For...** | Large enterprises needing deep, robust features. | Companies already using the Microsoft ecosystem. | Startups and developers focused on data and cost. |
